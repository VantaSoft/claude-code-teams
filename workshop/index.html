Build-A-Bot Workshop

Build your own team of AI agents in the next two hours — reachable from your phone.

Use the → button below (or your right arrow key) to move forward.

First: what kind of laptop are you on?

This page remembers your choice and your place — even if you close it or reboot. You can switch later from the top-right corner.

Today's destination

A persistent AI chief of staff

  • Runs on your laptop — you message it from your phone like a human assistant
  • Has access to your email and CRM — watches your calendar, runs recurring tasks on a schedule
  • Can spin up specialist agents (research, finance, marketing…) on request

Three steps to get there: a Claude plan → Claude Code → Claude Code Teams.

Step 0 · Windows only

Install WSL (Linux on Windows)

Already have Ubuntu/WSL set up? Skip ahead →

1. Open PowerShell as Administrator (right-click the Start button → Terminal (Admin), or Windows PowerShell (Admin) on Windows 10), then run:

wsl --install

2. When it finishes, reboot your laptop. This page remembers where you left off — just come back to cct.vantasoft.com/workshop.

3. After the reboot, an Ubuntu window opens (or launch “Ubuntu” from the Start menu). Create a username and password when it asks.

From here on, run every command in that Ubuntu window — not in PowerShell.

Step 1 · Account

Get a Claude plan

Already paying for Claude Pro or Max? Skip ahead →

  • Go to claude.ai and sign up (or log in)
  • Pro — $20/mo: the minimum plan that includes Claude Code. Fine for today.
  • Max — from $100/mo: get this if you want your agent team working for you all day, every day

The free plan does not include Claude Code.

Step 2 · Install Claude Code

Install Claude Code

Does claude --version already print a version in Terminal? Skip ahead →

Open Terminal (press ⌘-space, type “terminal”, hit return) and run:

curl -fsSL https://claude.ai/install.sh | bash

Then start it once to log in — a browser window opens; sign in with your claude.ai account:

claude

Logged in? Type /exit to leave — next step relaunches it as your team.

Step 2 · Install Claude Code

Install Claude Code (inside Ubuntu)

Does claude --version already print a version in Ubuntu? Skip ahead →

In your Ubuntu window (not PowerShell), run:

curl -fsSL https://claude.ai/install.sh | bash

Then start it once to log in — a browser window opens; sign in with your claude.ai account:

claude

Logged in? Type /exit to leave — next step relaunches it as your team.

Step 3 · Install Claude Code Teams

Install your team

In Terminal, run these four lines (one paste — the copy button grabs all of them):

cd ~
curl -fsSL https://cct.vantasoft.com/install.sh | bash
cd claude-code-teams/agents/orchestrator
claude --dangerously-skip-permissions

That last flag lets your agents act without asking approval for every step — it's what makes an autonomous team possible.

First conversation

Say hi — the wizard takes over

  • Type anything (try hi) — your orchestrator wakes up and walks you through the rest itself
  • It will ask your name, what to call it, and how you want to reach it
  • Keep the terminal window open — closing it stops your team (on Windows, the Ubuntu window is your team's home)

From here on there are no manuals — your orchestrator is the guide. Just tell it what you want.

Step 4 · Telegram

Set up your Telegram channel

  • Install the Telegram app on your phone (App Store / Google Play)
  • When the wizard asks Telegram or Slack: say Telegram
  • Work with the orchestrator from there — it walks you through creating your bot and connecting it

When it's done, you'll message your team from your phone like any other chat.

Step 5 · Email & calendar

Connect your email and calendar

  • Message your orchestrator: “Connect my email and calendar”
  • It knows how — it walks you through sign-in and asks for anything it needs
  • Then try it: “What's on my calendar this week?” or “Any emails I should answer?”
Step 6 · CRM

Connect your CRM

  • Tell it what you use: “Integrate with my HubSpot” (or Salesforce, Pipedrive…)
  • It figures out the way in and asks you for what it needs
  • Then try it: “Which deals haven't moved in two weeks?”
After the workshop

Laptops sleep. Teams shouldn't.

  • Your laptop is today's classroom — when it sleeps, your agents pause
  • For an always-on team: a Mac mini at home or a cheap cloud VM
